Poltergeist: The History Behind The Movie

History of Poltergeist

The famous movie Poltergeist was based on the Hermann family of Long Island, New York, who claimed to be haunted by a poltergeist that made objects fly through their home. While the family didn’t believe these occurrences at first, blaming the paranormal activity on teenagers who allegedly had sneaked into their home, the same thing happened just 5 days later. Mr. Hermann saw the bottles in action, as they moved across his bathroom as if dragged by some unseen hand. The police were called, and a series of priests came to the home to bless it. The Hermann family eventually moved away, but claimed that the house was haunted. It was claimed to have been caused by the presence of the adolescent kids. To this day, these events are still unexplainable and remain a mystery. Poltergeists tend to start off as harmless, but could become very deadly the longer they stick around in a home.

Stephen King's Role in Poltergeist

The producer of Poltergeist, Steven Spielberg, originally wanted Stephen King to write the film. Stephen King is well known as “the master of horror,” since he is the author of famous books such as The Shining, It, Pet Sematary, and Cujo. Sadly, unfortunate timing kept this collaboration from happening. When Spielberg attempted to contact King with the offer, King was busy traveling via boat to England. Because this was in the early 1980s, there were no internet equipped cell phones, which would result in taking longer times to relay messages between the two. In the meantime, Spielberg got tired of waiting, so he recruited Grais and Victor to help write the film, who would return to write the sequel of Poltergeist, which was Poltergeist 2: The Other Side.
